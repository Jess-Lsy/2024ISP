A CROSSROAD WITH BORDERS

Within the economic and political contexts of the trade linking Dandong, China, and Sinuiju, North Korea, this ethnographic research project looks at the various multilingual signage along two intercrossing streets—Shiwei Rd and Erjing St. This cross-section holds major business offices near the city’s Customs House that deals with trade and investment on both sides of the border, as well as “Chosun Streets”, which is composed mainly of service-oriented local businesses established by Dandong’s longtime ethnic Korean Chinese residents. Notably, These confined areas are where about a quarter of the DPRK’s external economic trade is handled, and is a major center of the DPRK’s legal/illegal trade conducted under cover of Chinese relative freedom. Within these unique contexts emerge distinctive lived experiences, narratives, and dialogues, that are reflected within its language use. By visualizing the data collected from 800+ signages within the past decade. The goal is to invite the viewer to partake in an interactive journey of exploring this unique and complex intersection of contexts, explore the ways in which broader systems, factors, and influences are reflected in these visual components, and then bring these reflections into our own, or other similar spaces.
